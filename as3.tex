\textbf{\textit{Problem (Papoulis):Chapter 6,6.71}}\\
The random variables x and y are uniform in the interval (-1,1)and independent. Find the conditional density fr(r \mid M) \text { of the random variable,}\\ r=\sqrt{X^2+Y^2},where M = {r\leq 1}\\



\textbf{\textit{Solution:}}\\

The mass density in the square \mid x \mid,\\\mid y \mid \text{ of the xy  plane  equals  1/4};\\
hence,P(r{\leq1})=x/4 and\\

   P({r\leq}r,{r\leq1})=
 \left\{
\begin{array}{l}
  P{r\leqr}=\pi r^2 /4,r\leq1    \\
    \vspace{0.01cm}  \\
     P{r\leqr}=\pi/4,r>1\\
\end{array}\\

 \\
    Fr(r\mid M)
    \\=P({r\leq r ,M})/P({r\leq 1})=\left\{\begin{array}{l}
     r^2 ;r\leq1  \\
     \vspace{0.01cm}\\
     1 ;r>1\\
    
\end{array} \\
